Why are we doing this?

Founders carry a psychological burden that's mostly invisible — to investors, to employees, to family, often to themselves. Cofounder conflict, identity-merge with the company, money pressure, isolation, the relentless self-judgment. Most of it never gets named out loud.

This survey exists for two reasons:

Understanding. We want a clearer, evidence-based picture of how founders are actually doing — not curated, not LinkedIn-polished. Validated clinical instruments (PHQ-9, GAD-7, ASRS, AQ-10, MBI) give us a real signal instead of vibes.

Normalizing. When you see your own results next to other founders', the loneliest stuff stops feeling so unique. The quotes at the top of this page are real things real founders wrote when reaching out for help. You're not the outlier you think you are.

What does it measure?

Validated clinical screeners for depression (PHQ-9), anxiety (GAD-7), ADHD traits (ASRS), autism spectrum traits (AQ-10), and burnout (MBI-GS).

Plus founder-specific sections: life outlook, ambition, founder-specific challenges, cofounder relationship, personality, social support, help-seeking, medication, and substance use.

How anonymous is it, really?

Your responses are tied to a random access code, not your identity. If you leave an email for the report, it lives in a separate table with no join key to your responses — even we can't look you up by email.

Don't take our word for it. The database schema and the full source code on GitHub are open for inspection. The privacy hardening commit is 005_privacy_hardening.sql.

What do I get?

Your scores with severity bands, how you compare to the general population, and (when the cohort is large enough) how you compare to other founders who've taken the survey.

Optionally leave an email for a detailed report and information on coaching, retreats, and research updates.
